Patio Planning Starts with Layout

For patios, we spend time thinking about size, furniture space, traffic flow, door clearance, and how the finished slab relates to steps, lawn areas, and surrounding grade.

A patio should feel intentional when it is finished. That means planning for both function and appearance, not just pouring a rectangle in the backyard and hoping it works.

What Makes a Patio Hold Up Well

Patio concrete still needs stable base preparation, reinforcement, and proper pitch for drainage. Water should move away from the home instead of collecting against the foundation or near door thresholds.

We also think about how the slab edges meet grass, landscaping, or walkways so the finished project looks deliberate instead of patched in.

Concrete Patio FAQs

Can you install stamped or colored patios?

Yes. We can discuss decorative finish options when the project scope, layout, and desired look call for them.

How do you keep water away from the house?

Patio slope and surrounding grade are planned so runoff moves away from the home instead of pooling against the foundation.

Can you match a patio to nearby concrete?

We can review layout, finish approach, and transitions to help the new patio fit the rest of the property as cleanly as possible.

Planning a New Concrete Patio?

We can help with shape, finish, slope, and site preparation so the patio works well long after the pour.
