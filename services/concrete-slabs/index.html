Why Slab Work Is Different

Slab projects depend heavily on elevation control, base stability, and how the slab will interact with walls, doors, framing, vehicles, and future use of the space.

Because of that, slab planning is often more technical than a basic flatwork project.

What We Review on Slab Projects

Subgrade Condition

We look at the soil and whether excavation, fill, or additional preparation is needed before placement.

Finished Elevation

Slab height matters for framing, garage doors, entry thresholds, and overall drainage around the building.

Thickness & Reinforcement

The intended use of the slab helps determine the build approach and support requirements.

Access & Equipment

We plan the job around site access, available working room, and clean execution from prep through finish.

Stable Slabs Start with Stable Prep

When a slab is supporting a building or carrying regular load, the prep work matters just as much as the concrete itself. We focus on grade, compaction, and consistent preparation so the slab has a solid foundation before it is poured.

That approach helps reduce settlement concerns and creates a better base for whatever the slab is meant to support.
